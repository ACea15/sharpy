SHARPy

SHARPy is an aeroelastic analysis package developed at the Department of Aeronautics, Imperial College London. It can be used for the structural, aerodynamic and aeroelastic analysis of flexible aircraft, flying wings and wind turbines.

Download

Nonlinear Aeroelasticity

At its core, SHARPy includes a geometrically-exact, displacement-based beam model that is coupled to an Unsteady Vortex Lattice Method, enabling the time domain simulation of nonlinear, highly flexible aeroelastic systems such as flexible aircraft and wind turbines.

Modular Framework

SHARPy employs a modular framework where the user can order solvers in different ways to simulate nearly endless variations of the same problem. Choose from nonlinear or linear modules, apply model order reduction techniques, analyse frequency responses or plot full time domain simulations.

Open-source

For you to explore, simulate, learn and contribute. You will find SHARPy on GitHub. We welcome any contribution you may want to make to the project: from tests, to examples to documentation and even new features! Please check our Contributing page.

Capabilities

SHARPy offers numerous analysis and simulation capabilities. As a small overview:

  • Static aerodynamic, structural and aeroelastic solutions
  • Finding trim conditions for aeroelastic configurations
  • Nonlinear, dynamic time domain simulations under a large number of conditions such as:
    • Prescribed trajectories.
    • Free flight.
    • Dynamic follower forces.
    • Control inputs in thrust, control surface deflection...
    • Arbitrary time-domain gusts, including non span-constant ones.
    • Full 3D turbulent fields.
  • Multibody dynamics with hinges, articulations and prescribed nodal motions.
    • Applicable to wind turbines.
    • Hinged aircraft.
    • Catapult assisted takeoffs.
  • Linear analysis
    • Linearisation around a nonlinear equilibrium.
    • Frequency response analysis.
    • Asymptotic stability analysis.
  • Model order reduction
    • Krylov-subspace reduction methods.
    • Balancing reduction methods.
